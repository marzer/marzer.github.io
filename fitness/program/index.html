The program

v1.0 — 2026-07-19

Three rules that outrank everything else:

  1. The knee's next-morning verdict — not how the run felt — decides progression.
  2. All running is conversational, HR ≤ ~145, cadence ≥ 170, flat routes, never on consecutive days (~72 h apart).
  3. When in doubt, do the boring smaller thing. The engine returns in weeks; tendons take months. The gap between those two is where the last five years happened.

Phase 0 — weeks 1–2

Run ladder and prehab start immediately; strength starts when the dumbbells arrive (until then: push-ups, tempo bodyweight squats, backpack good-mornings as placeholders). Record baselines: waist at navel, resting HR, step-down tolerance, and rung-1 knee response. First strength session: pick loads that allow 15+ reps, do 10–12. Log everything from day one.

Strength A — Mon

# Exercise Sets × reps
1 Goblet squat (pain-free depth only) 3 × 8–12
2 DB floor press 3 × 8–12
3 One-arm DB row (brace on any sturdy surface) 3 × 10–12 /side
4 DB RDL 3 × 10–12
5 Suitcase carry — hallway/stairwell laps 3 × 40 m
6 Optional finisher: lateral raises 2 × 12–15

Strength B — Fri

# Exercise Sets × reps
1 DB reverse lunge 3 × 8–10 /side
2 Standing DB overhead press 3 × 8–12
3 DB pullover, floor, hips bridged (at outdoor gym: chin-ups instead) 3 × 10–12
4 Single-leg hip thrust, shoulders on sofa 3 × 10–15 /side
5 Side plank 3 × 30–45″ /side
6 Optional finisher: hammer curls ⇄ overhead triceps 2 × 10–15

Progression (both days): stop every set ~2 reps shy of failure. When all sets hit the top of the range, next increment, restart at the bottom. When goblet squats cap out at max dumbbell weight: paused reps → two-DB front squat. Squat depth is earned back gradually as the knee allows, not forced.

Run ladder

Both weekly runs at the same rung; advance at most one rung per week. 5′ brisk walk before and after every run.

8 30′ continuous 30′

Knee score, logged the morning after every run:

Score Meaning
0 silent
1 mild awareness, gone same day
2 discomfort into the next day, or stairs noticeable
3 altered gait, or anything lasting 48 h+

Gate (on the week's worse score): 0–1 → advance next week · 2 → repeat the rung · 3 → down two rungs, extra rest day, and if a rung produces a 3 twice, stop and re-plan rather than grind.

Prehab block — after every run, ~12′

Banded lateral walks 2 × 15/side (side-lying hip abduction until the band arrives) · slow step-downs on a stair, 3″ lowering, 2 × 8/side · wall-sit 2 × 30–45″ · straight-leg calf raises off a stair edge 2 × 15–20. The step-downs double as the weekly knee assessment — same step, same tempo, compare week to week.

Deload

Every 6th week, or early if lifts stall while sleep/motivation degrade: one set off every exercise, hold the run rung but cut durations ~30%. Scheduled recovery is what makes 39 feel like a detail rather than a diagnosis.

Phase 2 — entry criteria, not a date

Two consecutive weeks at rung 8 with knee ≤ 1 → base runs grow toward 40′, one run gains 4–6 × 20″ strides (walk-back recovery) to reintroduce turnover, and in decent weather one strength day can migrate to the outdoor gym barbell for heavier hinging. Speed and volume goals get set then, from the data, not now.
